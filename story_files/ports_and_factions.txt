CARIBBEAN PORTS & FACTIONS (Late 1600s–Early 1700s)

ENGLISH / ROYAL NAVY:
- Port Royal (Jamaica): Trade hub, corrupt governors, taverns, smugglers.
- Nassau / New Providence: Pirate haven turned Royal Navy target.

SPANISH / NEW SPAIN:
- Cartagena: Well-fortified, treasure fleets pass nearby.
- Vera Cruz: High-value port, guarded but vulnerable from sea.
- Havana: Major shipyard and military center.

FRENCH:
- Tortuga: Iconic pirate base, loose law, privateers, rum.
- Saint-Domingue: Plantation wealth, opportunistic merchants.

DUTCH:
- Curaçao: Open trade port, spies, smugglers.

AMERICAN COLONIES (EMERGING):
- Charleston: Trade and politics, English influence.

NOTES:
- Spanish treasure routes run between Cartagena → Havana → Spain.
- Privateers vary loyalty based on bribes, letters of marque, or grudges.
