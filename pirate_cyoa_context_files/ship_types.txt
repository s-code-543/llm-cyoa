SHIP TYPES OF THE GOLDEN AGE OF PIRACY

Sloop — Fast, agile, light guns. Ideal for scouting and small raids.
Brig — Balanced speed and firepower. Common pirate choice.
Schooner — Very fast in favorable winds, light armament.
Frigate — Strong, disciplined naval vessel. Dangerous opponent.
Galleon — Treasure carriers. Slow, heavily built, guarded.
Man-of-War — Heavy cannons, elite crews. Avoid unless desperate.

NAVAL TERMS:
Fathoms (depth)
Cables (short distance)
Leagues (long distance)
Astern, abaft, bow, stern, lee side, windward, close-hauled, tacking, etc.
